Classic Apple Pie Recipe



Ingredients

  • 6-8 medium apples (Granny Smith or Honeycrisp)
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 3/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/4 cup brown sugar (packed)
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch
  • 2 teaspoons vanilla extract
  • 2 tablespoons butter, cut into small pieces
  • 1 package (2 crusts) refrigerated pie dough
  • 1 tablespoon milk or cream (for brushing the crust)
  • 1 tablespoon coarse sugar (for sprinkling on top)

Steps

  1. Prepare the Apples

    Peel, core, and slice the apples into thin wedges (about 1/4-inch thick).
    Toss the apple slices in a bowl with lemon juice to prevent browning.

  2. Make the Filling

    In a separate bowl, combine the granulated sugar, brown sugar, salt, cinnamon, nutmeg, flour, and cornstarch.
    Add the dry mixture to the apple slices and toss to coat evenly. Stir in the vanilla extract.
    Let the apple mixture sit for 10 minutes while you prepare the crust.

  3. Prepare the Pie Crust

    Roll out one pie crust and place it into a 9-inch pie pan, gently pressing it into the edges.
    Pour the apple filling into the crust, spreading it evenly.
    Dot the filling with butter pieces.

  4. Top the Pie

    Roll out the second pie crust and place it over the apples.
    Trim any excess dough, leaving about an inch around the edges.
    Crimp the edges to seal, and make a few slits on the top to let steam escape.
    Brush the top with milk or cream and sprinkle with coarse sugar.

  5. Bake the Pie

    Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C).
    Place the pie on a baking sheet (to catch any drips) and bake for 45–50 minutes or until the crust is golden brown and the filling is bubbling.
    If the edges brown too quickly, cover them with foil.

  6. Cool and Serve

    Let the pie cool for at least 1 hour before slicing to let the filling set.
    Serve with vanilla ice cream or whipped cream for an extra treat!
